### Know your customer — KYC corporate onboarding

**Actors:**
- Henrik — CFO, NordRail Systems GmbH (representative)
- NordRail Systems GmbH — account applicant (primary)
- Continental Bank Vienna branch — financial institution (verifier)
- Bundesanzeiger Verlag, DATEV, Dutch KVK, Dutch Tax Agency — credential issuers
- NordRail's Dutch subsidiary — linked entity

**Preconditions:** NordRail holds a European Business Wallet with legal-entity, EUCC, LEI, VAT, and tax credentials. Henrik holds an EU Digital Identity Wallet with a personal identity credential and a board-issued EU Power of Attorney. Continental Bank's portal supports mutual authentication and policy-driven attestation requests.

**Trigger:** NordRail wins a multi-year Austrian rail contract and must open a dedicated project account at Continental Bank's Vienna branch to manage contract receipts and payments.

**Postconditions:** Continental Bank holds a structured, machine-verifiable AML/KYC record for NordRail and Henrik. Henrik's PoA is linked to the account and can be revoked and replaced without restarting the full KYC process for the company.

---

Henrik is the CFO of NordRail Systems GmbH, a Hamburg-based manufacturer of railway signalling equipment. NordRail has won a multi-year contract to supply and commission signalling infrastructure for an Austrian rail operator and needs to open a dedicated project current account with Continental Bank's Vienna branch to manage contract receipts, subcontractor payments, and project-related foreign exchange. Opening a corporate account triggers a full KYC process under the Austrian implementation of the EU Anti-Money Laundering Directive: the bank must verify NordRail's legal identity, confirm its beneficial ownership structure, establish its tax registration in Germany, validate its VAT status, and verify the identity and authority of the individual presenting on the company's behalf.

Henrik is not NordRail's registered statutory director. He is the chief financial officer, authorised by a board resolution to open and manage bank accounts on the company's behalf. Continental Bank's onboarding team informs him that a notarised power of attorney confirming his authority must accompany the application, together with a certified extract from the German commercial register, a UBO declaration in a format prescribed by the Austrian financial market authority, a certificate of tax registration issued by the German Finanzamt, and a VAT registration confirmation. All German-language documents must be accompanied by certified Austrian-standard translations.

The certified commercial register extract must be requested from Bundesanzeiger Verlag and typically takes five to seven business days to process. The UBO declaration must reproduce the ownership chain in a specific Austrian template that does not correspond to the German UBO register's standard output format, so it must be reconstructed manually by NordRail's legal counsel. The Finanzamt issues tax registration certificates by post. The notarised and apostilled power of attorney requires a visit to a German notary, then submission to the relevant state authority for apostillage, adding a further ten days and several hundred euros in fees. By the time all documents reach Vienna, the commercial register extract is already close to its sixty-day freshness limit recognised by the bank's compliance team.

Continental Bank's compliance officer also notes that NordRail's Dutch subsidiary — registered separately with the Dutch KVK — is listed as a co-signatory on the Austrian contract. The bank's internal policy engine flags that the ownership link between the German parent and the Dutch subsidiary must be evidenced before the account can be opened for project receipts that will flow through both entities. This requirement was not communicated at the start of the process. A second documentation round begins.

What Henrik and Continental Bank's compliance team both need is a mechanism through which NordRail presents its organisational identity, ownership structure, tax standing, and the authority of its representative in a single verifiable interaction — machine-checkable against the authoritative sources, current at the moment of presentation, and legally equivalent across German and Austrian jurisdictions without requiring translation or notarisation.

NordRail loads its European Business Wallet with credentials obtained directly from the relevant authentic sources. Bundesanzeiger Verlag issues a legal-entity credential carrying NordRail's Legal Entity Identifier and its EUCC company attestation, and assigns the company its EBWOID — the wallet's own verifiable identifier within the EU trust framework. DATEV, acting as the interface to the German fiscal authority, issues a tax-number credential and a VAT registration credential for the German entity. The Dutch subsidiary's credentials — issued by the KVK and the Dutch Tax Agency — are held in a linked wallet profile and accessible under a sub-presentation scoped to the subsidiary's identifier.

NordRail's board issues a EU Power of Attorney credential into Henrik's EU Digital Identity Wallet. The credential names Henrik by reference to his personal identity credential, specifies his authority to open and manage bank accounts on NordRail's behalf, and is signed by NordRail's Business Wallet. It carries a defined validity period and a revocation endpoint controlled by NordRail's authorised signatories. Henrik does not hold a copy of the credential as a document; he holds it as a cryptographic object bound to his personal wallet.

When Henrik initiates the account opening with Continental Bank's Vienna portal, the portal presents its own verified identity to Henrik's wallet before requesting any data — mutual authentication establishes that he is interacting with a genuine authorised institution, not a spoofed onboarding page. Henrik's wallet presents NordRail's organisation base data in the first exchange: the EUCC, LEI, EBWOID, VAT number, and tax number credentials, drawn from Bundesanzeiger Verlag and DATEV respectively. Continental Bank's internal policy engine processes the base data, applies its AML risk classification rules, and determines that the account type and contract value require beneficial ownership disclosure and confirmation of the representative's authority. A scoped presentation request is returned to Henrik's wallet, specifying the UBO credential and the EU Power of Attorney credential as additionally required. The request states the purpose and the legal basis for each data element requested.

Henrik reviews the request in his wallet, confirms that the disclosure scope is consistent with what was described during the onboarding initiation, and releases the additional credentials. Continental Bank's verification system resolves each issuer's identifier — Bundesanzeiger Verlag for the legal-entity and ownership data, DATEV for the tax credentials, KVK and the Dutch Tax Agency for the subsidiary link — against the European Business Register and the EU trusted-authority registry. Every signature is verified. Every status endpoint is queried in real time, confirming that none of the credentials have been revoked or lapsed since issuance. The ownership chain from the German parent to the Dutch subsidiary is confirmed through the linked wallet presentation without requiring a separate documentation exercise.

Henrik's EU Power of Attorney credential is verified against his personal identity credential: the bank confirms that the natural person standing at the presentation endpoint is the same individual named in the authorisation, and that NordRail's wallet — not a third party — issued the mandate. No notary is involved. No apostille is required. The bank stores a structured audit record of each credential presented, each issuer resolved, and each status check performed, satisfying its AML documentation obligations without holding copies of the underlying source documents.

The bank's compliance officer notes that an employee and contact person credential — identifying Henrik as the ongoing account management contact for operational correspondence — has also been included in the presentation. This is stored in the customer master data record alongside the verified organisational credentials, giving the bank a point of contact that is verifiably linked to NordRail's authenticated identity rather than entered as free text on a form.

Three months into the account relationship, NordRail restructures its board and revokes Henrik's power of attorney. The revocation is recorded in the credential's status endpoint. When Continental Bank's periodic re-verification cycle runs the following quarter, the EU PoA credential for Henrik returns a revoked status. The bank flags the account for updated authorisation documentation. NordRail's new authorised signatory loads a fresh EU PoA credential into their own wallet and completes the re-authorisation through the same wallet presentation flow, without restarting the full KYC process for the company itself.
