### Trusted AI for energy grid forecasting and automated flexibility activation

**Actors:**
- Miriam — grid operations lead, RheinNetz DSO (primary)
- RheinNetz AI forecasting agent — authorised grid agent
- NordTSO — transmission system operator
- BalanceOne GmbH — balancing group manager
- FlexPool Energy GmbH — flexibility operator
- FlexPool AI bidding agent — counterparty agent

**Preconditions:** RheinNetz, NordTSO, BalanceOne, and FlexPool each hold Business Wallets with legal person identity and market role credentials issued by the relevant energy market authority. RheinNetz has issued a scoped Agent PoA and AISP for its forecasting agent. FlexPool has issued an Agent PoA for its bidding agent with an agent validation credential.

**Trigger:** RheinNetz's AI forecasting agent detects a predicted congestion event in a defined grid area and prepares a flexibility activation request directed at FlexPool's flexibility assets.

**Postconditions:** FlexPool has received and responded to a scoped flexibility activation request. All steps are logged with legal person identity, market role, Agent PoA reference, AISP version, validation status, and policy decision. High-impact activations have required control-room approval. A suspended agent validation credential blocks automated activation until restored.

---

Miriam is responsible for grid operations at RheinNetz, a German distribution system operator. RheinNetz manages a regional grid with rooftop PV, heat pumps, EV chargers, home batteries, commercial battery storage, and flexible industrial loads. Some days have too much local PV generation. Other days have peak loads from EV charging and heat pumps.

RheinNetz uses an AI forecasting service to predict grid congestion, voltage issues, and flexibility needs. The AI service uses weather forecasts, smart meter data, grid topology data, battery telemetry, EV charging forecasts, and flexibility offers. NordTSO, a transmission system operator, needs selected forecasts for system coordination. BalanceOne GmbH, a balancing group manager, needs schedule updates. FlexPool Energy GmbH, a flexibility operator, offers batteries, EV fleets, heat pumps, and industrial loads.

Today, each party uses its own onboarding process, API credentials, data-sharing contracts, and manual role checks. RheinNetz must verify whether FlexPool is authorised to offer flexibility in a grid area. NordTSO must know whether a forecast was generated by an approved AI service. FlexPool must know whether an activation request came from an authorised DSO or TSO process. AI agents can help, but the market actors cannot safely rely on them without verifiable identity, role, mandate, validation, and audit evidence.

What the ecosystem needs is a verifiable trust framework for AI-assisted energy market processes. Every legal person must be identifiable. Every market role must be machine-verifiable. Every AI agent must act under a scoped PoA. Every AI service must have validation evidence. Every forecast and activation must be attributable, auditable, and revocable.

RheinNetz, NordTSO, BalanceOne, and FlexPool each load their Business Wallets with legal person identity credentials. They also receive market role authorisation credentials from the relevant authentic source or authorised market role registry. RheinNetz receives a DSO role credential. NordTSO receives a TSO role credential. BalanceOne receives a balancing group manager credential. FlexPool receives a flexibility operator credential. Each credential includes the market role, jurisdiction, validity period, identifier, and status endpoint.

RheinNetz issues a scoped Agent PoA to its grid forecasting agent. The PoA authorises the agent to request input data, generate short-term forecasts, publish congestion forecasts, and prepare flexibility requests for defined grid areas. It does not authorise high-impact activation without control-room approval. RheinNetz also issues an AI Service Passport for the forecasting service. The AISP contains the model version, input data classes, grid area scope, forecast horizon, TEVV results, fallback rules, human oversight controls, incident records, and post-release validation evidence.

FlexPool issues an Agent PoA to its flexibility bidding agent. The PoA authorises the agent to submit flexibility offers, update asset availability, and accept low-risk activation requests within agreed limits. FlexPool’s optimisation service holds an agent validation credential. FlexPool also holds NIST AI RMF and ISO/IEC 42001 operator credentials.

Before RheinNetz’s forecasting agent requests asset data from FlexPool, both agents perform mutual A2A authentication. RheinNetz presents its legal person credential, DSO role credential, Agent PoA, AISP, and validation credential. FlexPool verifies the credentials and checks that the request is inside the grid area and market process. FlexPool then presents its legal person credential, flexibility operator credential, Agent PoA, asset availability credential, and validation credential.

When the forecast shows a likely congestion event, RheinNetz’s agent prepares a flexibility activation request. The request includes the grid area, time window, requested flexibility volume, technical constraints, market role basis, and forecast reference. Low-risk cases can proceed automatically. High-impact cases require control-room approval. Every step is logged with the legal person identity, market role, agent identity, PoA, AISP version, validation status, timestamp, and policy decision.

Later, a validation body suspends the validation credential for FlexPool’s optimisation agent because a software update was deployed without post-release validation. RheinNetz detects the suspension during the next A2A verification. Automated activation is blocked until a valid agent validation credential is restored.

**Requirements:** Authentication/proof of control, legal person identity, DSO market role credential, TSO market role credential, balancing group manager credential, flexibility operator credential, market-role authorisation credential for regulated processes, Agent PoA, AI Service Passport, agent validation credential, NIST AI RMF operator credential, ISO/IEC 42001 operator credential, machine-to-machine data exchange, A2A mutual authentication, policy-based access control, selective disclosure, real-time credential status and revocation, verifiable audit trail, runtime monitoring, human override, forecast provenance, flexibility activation provenance, cannot be administratively denied.
