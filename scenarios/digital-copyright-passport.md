### Digital Copyright Passport for IP creators and licensors

**Actors:**
- Sofia — independent concept artist, Barcelona (natural person, creator and licensor)
- NexGen Studios — independent game publisher, Warsaw (legal person, licensee)
- Creator Trust Domain — creator membership management organisation (trusted registry)
- Business registry and LPID issuer — EU member-state identity authority
- EUIPO, customs authorities, and media marketplaces — verifiers

**Preconditions:** Sofia holds a Personal Identity (PID) credential in her EUDI Wallet, which she has inherited into a Business Wallet (EUBW) to access professional IP management features. NexGen Studios holds a Business Wallet with a legal person identity credential and a KYB credential. A Creator Trust Domain operated by a recognised collective rights organisation maintains a trusted registry of creators, their onboarding timestamps, and the designated blockchain (EBSI) for anchoring NFTs and licensing transactions.

**Trigger:** Sofia completes a set of original concept art illustrations for a fantasy game environment and wants to register her authorship, prevent unauthorised use, and offer the work for licensing through an IP marketplace without relying on a centralised copyright registry or a traditional rights management agency.

**Postconditions:** Each illustration is registered under a Digital Copyright Passport (DCP) consisting of an EUBW-signed verifiable authorship credential and an EBSI-anchored NFT identifier, linked to Sofia's verified creator identity. A DCAT-compliant IP catalogue entry makes the work discoverable to potential licensees. NexGen Studios holds an ODRL-encoded licence NFT recording the agreed terms, exclusivity, territory, royalty rate, and validity period. Any licensee, auditor, EUIPO officer, customs authority, or media marketplace can verify the full chain of authorship, licensing status, and rights in real time.

---

Sofia is a freelance concept artist based in Barcelona who creates original digital illustrations for the games industry. She sells non-exclusive and exclusive licences to independent and professional game studios across Europe and North America. Her work appears in printed box art, digital storefronts, in-game assets, and promotional campaigns. Each licensing engagement involves manual coordination: email exchanges to agree terms, PDF contracts with no machine-readable rights expressions, invoice reconciliation without an on-chain record, and no reliable way to establish authorship if a dispute arises later.

Today, the process is fragile. Downstream buyers, platforms, and authorities cannot easily verify who created a work, whether the person offering the licence has authority to do so, whether a licensed volume has been exceeded, whether rights were cleared before publication, or whether a competing licence has already been issued to another party. Counterfeit and unauthorised reproductions spread with no attribution trail. When licensing disputes arise — for example, a studio claiming exclusivity while a parallel use appears in another product — Sofia has no machine-readable evidence to present to an IP authority or court.

What Sofia needs is a Digital Copyright Passport: a verifiable, tamper-evident credential that captures her identity as the creator, timestamps the work at creation, records the full licensing history in a form that machines can evaluate, and can be inspected by licensees, marketplaces, customs authorities, and IP offices without requiring her direct involvement in every verification.

The foundation is a verified creator identity. Sofia's PID credential from her EUDI Wallet is inherited into a Business Wallet, enabling her to authenticate as a natural person, sign IP transactions, and present credentials to verifiers. For professional IP management she onboards with a **Creator Trust Domain** — a Creator Membership Management Organisation that maintains a trusted registry recording her identity, onboarding timestamp, and the designated blockchain (EBSI) for anchoring all her NFTs and licensing transactions. The membership credential issued at onboarding timestamps her active registration and designates EBSI as the single authoritative registry for her IP, preventing duplicate NFT creation across competing platforms. If Sofia later attempts to register the same work with a second membership organisation, the overlapping membership timestamps establish clear accountability and allow licensees to pursue legal recourse.

When Sofia finalises an illustration she computes an ISCC fingerprint — a content-derived identifier that is unique to the specific work and enables content matching across formats and resolutions. ([iscc.codes](https://iscc.codes/)) Her Business Wallet issues a verifiable authorship credential recording her DID, the ISCC fingerprint, the creation date, and the authorship assertion. This credential is anchored to the work through the ISCC identifier, binding the verifiable claim of origin to the content without dependence on any specific file container or embedded metadata format.

The Business Wallet combines the authorship credential with an on-chain NFT anchored on EBSI, forming the Digital Copyright Passport for the work. EBSI hosts Sofia's DID document, the Creator Trust Domain trust list, and the revocation registry, ensuring that all credentials and transactions can be verified by any relying party across EU member states without a central intermediary. The DCP is published as a DCAT-compliant catalogue entry in the IP catalogue, making the work discoverable by licensees and IP platforms while retaining Sofia's control over access and terms. ([ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu/digital-building-blocks/sites/display/EBSI))

When NexGen Studios discovers Sofia's work in the IP catalogue and requests a licence, the transaction is conducted through the Business Wallet. NexGen Studios presents its legal person identity credential and KYB credential. Sofia's Business Wallet issues an ODRL-encoded licence NFT recording the licensor DID, the licensee DID, a unique licence ID, the licensed asset identified by ISCC, the permitted use (game artwork, digital distribution), the territory (EU and North America), the exclusivity status, the licensed volume, the royalty rate, the validity period, and the issuing authority. ([www.w3.org](https://www.w3.org/TR/odrl-model/)) The licence NFT is anchored on EBSI and linked to the DCP, creating an auditable licensing record attached to the work itself. Royalty payments are automated by the smart contract: as NexGen Studios exceeds licensed volume thresholds, additional royalties are triggered and settled on-chain without manual reconciliation.

Any verifier — a marketplace operator, EUIPO officer, customs authority checking IP rights at a border crossing, or a competing studio asserting a prior claim — can inspect the DCP. The DCP shows who created the work and when, which Creator Trust Domain the creator belongs to, which licences have been issued, to whom, under what terms, and whether those licences remain valid. If NexGen Studios seeks to sublicense the work to a film studio, the verifier checks whether the ODRL licence NFT includes a sublicensing permission. If it does not, the downstream transaction fails the verification check before it completes. If a licence needs to be revoked — for example, because payment obligations were not met — Sofia's Business Wallet updates the revocation registry on EBSI and all relying platforms reflect the change immediately.

Later, Sofia discovers her illustrations appearing in a product she did not licence. She presents the DCP and the Creator Trust Domain registry entry as evidence. The DCP shows that no valid licence NFT was issued for that use. The absence of a corresponding ODRL licence in the EBSI registry, combined with the immutable creation timestamp and ISCC fingerprint, provides machine-readable evidence for EUIPO proceedings, customs enforcement, or civil litigation. The evidence chain cannot be altered retrospectively and cannot be administratively denied by any single party. If Sofia requires anonymity in a particular jurisdiction, she can conduct transactions through a registered third party without compromising the underlying transparency mechanisms or breaking the audit trail.
