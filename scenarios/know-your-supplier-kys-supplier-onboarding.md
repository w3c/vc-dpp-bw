### Know your supplier — KYS supplier onboarding

**Actors:**
- Claudia — head of supplier qualification, ViaRail AG (verifier)
- Enrico — commercial director, MetalWorks S.r.l. (subject)
- TÜV Italia, INPS, GS1 Italy, Dun & Bradstreet, Agenzia delle Entrate, Infocamere, MetalWorks' bank — credential issuers

**Preconditions:** MetalWorks holds a European Business Wallet with credentials from all required authorities. ViaRail's supplier portal supports mutual authentication and scoped presentation requests. MetalWorks' social security compliance credential carries a live status endpoint.

**Trigger:** ViaRail AG identifies MetalWorks S.r.l. as a candidate supplier for safety-critical rail components and sends a supplier registration link to its procurement portal.

**Postconditions:** MetalWorks is registered as an approved vendor in ViaRail's master data management system. Ongoing compliance — including the 90-day INPS renewal cycle — is monitored automatically. Partial re-qualification is possible without repeating the full onboarding process.

---

Claudia is the head of supplier qualification at ViaRail AG, a Swiss rail infrastructure operator responsible for maintaining several thousand kilometres of track across Switzerland and southern Germany. ViaRail is expanding its approved vendor list for safety-critical track fastening components and has identified MetalWorks S.r.l., an Italian steel fabricator based in Brescia, as a candidate supplier. Before MetalWorks can receive a purchase order, ViaRail's procurement policy requires a structured supplier onboarding check spanning legal identity, tax compliance, beneficial ownership, payment details, labour law adherence, supply chain identifiers, and a certified site attestation confirming that MetalWorks' manufacturing facility meets the standards required for rail-grade component production.

Claudia's team sends MetalWorks a supplier registration link to ViaRail's procurement portal. The form requests fifteen categories of documentation. MetalWorks must upload a certified extract from the Italian business register confirming its legal status, a UBO declaration identifying its ultimate beneficial owners, a certificate of tax regularity from the Agenzia delle Entrate, its Italian VAT registration, a letter from its bank confirming the IBAN to be used for payment settlement, its Dun & Bradstreet DUNS number, its GS1 Italy company prefix licence, a declaration of social security contribution compliance from INPS, and a site audit certificate from an accredited notified body confirming conformity with EN 13674, the relevant European rail component standard. Each document must be translated into German or French if it is not already available in one of those languages.

Enrico, MetalWorks' commercial director, recognises the request immediately. His company completed equivalent onboarding exercises with four other industrial customers across Germany, Austria, and France in the same calendar year. Each customer used a different portal format and accepted documents in different combinations of languages. The INPS social security compliance certificate expires every ninety days, meaning it had to be re-requested and re-uploaded for each customer engagement. The site audit certificate, issued by TÜV Italia after a two-day factory inspection, was submitted as a scanned PDF to each customer separately with no mechanism for any of them to verify that the scan matched the signed original. The total administrative effort across all five onboarding exercises exceeded sixty person-hours, and three of the five processes are still incomplete.

What Claudia and Enrico both need is a mechanism by which MetalWorks assembles its supplier compliance profile once as a set of machine-verifiable credentials, and presents the relevant subset to each new customer through a single wallet interaction — without re-collecting documents, re-requesting certificates, or duplicating translation effort.

MetalWorks loads its European Business Wallet with a set of credentials obtained directly from the authoritative sources. Infocamere, the Italian business register authority, issues a legal-entity credential carrying MetalWorks' Legal Entity Identifier, its EUCC company identity attestation, and its EBWOID wallet identifier. The Agenzia delle Entrate issues a tax-number credential and a VAT registration credential. The Italian UBO register issues a beneficial-ownership credential listing MetalWorks' ultimate beneficial owners. INPS issues a social security contribution compliance credential with a ninety-day validity window and a live status endpoint that reflects the current compliance status without requiring a new document to be requested. MetalWorks' bank issues an IBAN attestation credential, cryptographically binding the payment account to MetalWorks' verified legal-entity identifier. GS1 Italy issues a GS1 company prefix credential. Dun & Bradstreet issues a DUNS credential. TÜV Italia, as an accredited notified body, issues a site attestation credential confirming conformity of the Brescia facility with EN 13674, valid for three years and carrying a revocation endpoint.

When Enrico connects to ViaRail's supplier portal, he selects MetalWorks' Business Wallet as his authentication method. The portal initiates mutual authentication, confirming ViaRail's identity to MetalWorks before requesting any data. MetalWorks' wallet presents the organisation base data — legal-entity credential, EUCC, VAT, tax number — in the first exchange. ViaRail's internal policy engine processes the base data and determines that, for a supplier of safety-critical rail components, a site attestation and social security compliance credential are additionally required. A scoped presentation request is returned to Enrico's wallet specifying exactly which further credentials are needed and for what stated purpose. Enrico reviews the request in the wallet interface, confirms the presentation, and the remaining credentials are released. No credential beyond those listed in the policy engine's request is disclosed.

ViaRail's procurement platform receives the full presentation, resolves each issuer's identifier against the European Business Register, the GS1 registry, the Dun & Bradstreet authority endpoint, and the notified body registry, verifies every credential signature, and queries the live status endpoints for the INPS compliance credential and the TÜV site attestation. The IBAN is verified as cryptographically bound to MetalWorks' legal-entity identifier. Every check resolves without manual intervention. MetalWorks is written into ViaRail's master data management system as an approved vendor within the same session. Claudia's team is notified automatically.

When the ninety-day INPS compliance window expires three months later, the status endpoint for MetalWorks' social security credential returns a lapsed status. ViaRail's platform flags the supplier record for re-verification. MetalWorks requests a renewed compliance credential from INPS, loads it into its wallet, and completes a targeted re-presentation of that single credential to each affected customer without repeating the full onboarding process.

When MetalWorks is acquired by a larger industrial group the following year and its bank account changes, Enrico uses the wallet to issue an updated IBAN attestation from the new banking relationship. ViaRail's accounts payable system detects that the IBAN in the new credential differs from the one on record and requires Claudia to explicitly approve the change before the payment routing is updated — a control that prevents undetected account substitution of the kind that has affected several of ViaRail's peers.
